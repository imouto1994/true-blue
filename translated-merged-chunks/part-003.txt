■ Fuyuki Episode 04 ~n
……Now, what should I do?
[Choices]
1. 　I can see Aoi again tomorrow
2. 　Aoi is more important than a drama
$Akito
“……This drama is actually pretty interesting.”
After my bath, I wasn’t quite ready for bed yet, so I was watching TV in the living room.
I hadn't decided on anything specific to watch; I figured if I turned the TV on and there was nothing but boring programs, I’d just shut it off immediately.
But that thought had vanished far into the distance.
It was a drama that people in my class had been talking about, and just as the rumors suggested, it’s surprisingly good.
The protagonists are a tall male detective and his relatively beautiful female assistant.
The two of them go about scientifically unraveling various occult phenomena.
Well, it could be called an ambitious work that skillfully incorporates occult elements into the standard detective genre.
Despite being broadcast in a late-night slot, it now boasts immense popularity across a wide range of audiences.
So far, three series exist in total, and what’s currently airing is actually a rebroadcast of the first series.
Apparently, in conjunction with a movie being released this winter, all three series are being rebroadcast all at once.
I’m not usually the type of guy who watches dramas and such, but this one is fun.
In addition to the manzai-style banter between the two lead actors, there are plenty of moments where the supporting cast’s visual tricks are actually quite hilarious.
$Akito
“Man…… I think I’m gonna get hooked on this.”
Just then, the doorbell rang.
$Akito
“What? ……At this hour…?”
For a second, I doubted my own ears.
Given the hour, and the fact that the drama was just getting to the good part, the visitor had some seriously bad timing.
$Akito
“If someone’s coming over at this time…… it’s gotta be Aoi.”
Considering what happened this evening, it would be a lie to say I wasn't worried about how Aoi was doing.
Part of me really wanted to go to the front door, but……
$Akito
“Well, Big Bro or someone else will probably get it anyway…… so whatever!”
The story had just reached its climax.
I can see Aoi again tomorrow anyway! But if I miss the drama once, who knows when I’ll get the chance to see it again.
$Akito
“This is great! Definitely a must-watch!”
I rang the doorbell at the Kurusu residence.
$Aoi
“……I wonder if he’s home?”
Usually, Akito would come running and open the door right away, but…… today, I didn't come just to see Akito.
After a short while, the front door opened with a clicking sound.
The person who came out was Fuyuki-oniichan.
$Aoi
“Good evening, oniichan.”
$Fuyuki
“Ah, good evening. Is it Akito you're looking for?”
$Aoi
“No, not today. I thought I'd give this to you, oniichan……”
Saying that, I showed Fuyuki-oniichan a small parcel.
$Fuyuki
“What’s this……?”
I placed the package—wrapped in pale blue paper and tied with a yellow ribbon—plop into his hand.
$Aoi
“A thank-you for today. Because you helped me.”
I said to Fuyuki-oniichan with a bright smile.
He smiled back, looking a little bashful.
$Fuyuki
“Ahaha, thank you. I didn't really do anything that big, though……”
$Aoi
“Ehehe.”
$Fuyuki
“May I open it?”
$Aoi
“Yeah, go ahead. ‘It’s just a little something,’ as they say.”
Fuyuki-oniichan hurriedly began to unwrap the package right there.
Then, he pulled out some small cookies wrapped in clear plastic.
$Fuyuki
“Heh, cookies. Are these, by any chance……”
$Aoi
“Yeah. They’re homemade.”
$Fuyuki
“They look delicious. I’ll enjoy them, thank you.”
When Fuyuki-oniichan said that, I felt so happy I couldn't help but smile.
$Fuyuki
“Say, Aoi-chan. Akito is here too, why don't you come in?”
Oniichan pointed inside the house, trying to invite me in.
But I decided to decline.
$Aoi
“No, I’ll pass for today. I just came to deliver that.”
$Fuyuki
“I see……”
Fuyuki-oniichan looked a little disappointed.
Feeling slightly bad about it, I gave him a small wave.
$Aoi
“Well then, goodnight, oniichan. See you tomorrow.”
$Fuyuki
“Yeah, goodnight.”
Oniichan waved back at me and quietly closed the door.
I waited to make sure the door was locked, then spun around and leaned my back against the door.
Suddenly feeling overwhelmed with bashfulness, I let out a little giggle.
$Aoi
“I’m so glad he accepted them……”
The bad things that happened today were already forgotten; I was filled with a sense of joy.
I hope this happy feeling lasts forever.
While I was agonizing over it, the doorbell rang again.
I can't keep them waiting any longer.
Anyway, it’s a popular show that’s already been released on video and DVD.
There will probably be other chances to see it, and missing one or two episodes isn't something to get hung up on.
With that thought, I stood up from the sofa and hurried toward the entrance.
Reaching the front door, I opened it immediately.
Aoi was standing on the other side of the door.
$Aoi
“Good evening, Akito.”
$Akito
“What, it really is Aoi.”
$Aoi
“Hmph, ‘what’ is quite a greeting……”
Saying that, Aoi pouted slightly. For some reason, I found myself thinking she was cute.
It seems she's her usual self. No lingering effects from this evening, then.
$Akito
“No point standing around talking, come on in.”
$Aoi
“I’d love to come in, but I’ll pass today.”
$Akito
“Eh, why?”
$Aoi
“Because I only came to give you this today.”

It was a beautiful parcel, wrapped in pale blue paper and a yellow ribbon.
I knew immediately that it was something Aoi had made herself.
$Aoi
“It’s a thank-you. Could you give it to Fuyuki-oniichan?”
$Akito
“Eh? Ah, sure, but……”
What the hell…… I helped too, so is there nothing for me?
……Crap, sulking like this isn't like me.

$Akito
“(Oh man, I’ve got one of those faces that gives everything away immediately……)”

“Don't worry, there’s plenty for Akito too.”

It was wrapped in pale lime-green paper and tied with a ribbon that was purple…… or rather, lavender-colored.
$Akito

$Aoi
“Just so you know……”
$Akito
“Hm?”

$Akito
“Hahaha……”

$Aoi
“Well then, see you tomorrow.”
$Akito

For some reason, I felt a sense of warmth.
I felt as though I might just float away.
